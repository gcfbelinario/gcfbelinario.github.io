I come from the northern plains of Isabela province – my beloved land free from the frequencies of the cities, the birthplace of my worldy ruminations.

My formal education in physics began at the University of the Philippines Diliman, where I earned my Bachelor of Science in Applied Physics from the National Institute of Physics. In my undergraduate, I trained in computational physics and data-driven astrophysics under the mentorship of Reina Reyes. I pursued my Master's degree afterwards at the same institution, working under the wings of Reggie Bernardo and Ian Vega. Across both programs, my research focused on exploring the potential of gravitational waves as a tool in understanding gravitational lensing phenomena and cosmology.

Physicists are foremost, human beings that also long for indulgence. In my free time, I enjoy reading mangas and fictional novels, playing online games (i.e. GI, WuWav) and card games (i.e. MTG) with my colleagues!

Interests


I pursue interesting problems in astrophysics, cosmology, and computational physics. In particular, I work on gravitational waves, gravitational lensing, dark energy, and applications of machine learning in these fields.
